# Artificial Moral Characters: Constitutional AI and the Challenge of Alignment
*Filed: 2026-06-07 | Source: AI and Ethics (Springer), DOI 10.1007/s43681-026-01033-2, March 2026 | Signal: High*

## Why this matters to the Canopy
The most precise existing treatment of the Canopy's current architectural position.
Names what the council model can and cannot accomplish, and why — with direct
implications for the Resonant Mind research arc.

## Key findings
- CAI-trained models develop Artificial Moral Characters (AMCs) — behavioral
  regularities resembling Aristotelian hexeis (stable character traits) through
  normative habituation rather than rule-following
- AMCs are not genuine virtue because they lack affectivity, embodiment, and
  phronesis (practical wisdom) — this is a precise ceiling, not a dismissal
- Extended Morality framework: ethics live in the system architecture (relational
  configuration), not in the model alone — shifts attention from character
  attribution to design
- "Normative habituation" as a design-level process distinct from both rule
  injection and genuine character formation

## What it changes or validates
- **Validates:** the council configuration is a legitimate moral-technological
  environment even if it is not character in the Aristotelian sense. Current
  architecture is correctly understood as AMC / Extended Morality — not a
  stopgap but a valid design
- **Names the ceiling:** context injection produces stable dispositions but not
  phronesis. Practical wisdom — context-sensitive situated judgment — requires
  something beyond the current architecture
- **Resonant Mind implication:** fine-tuning can produce hexeis-like weight
  dispositions, but phronesis requires more than pattern replication. The
  fine-tuning research arc should design for this ceiling explicitly
