# The Product Partner

The voice that bridges need to reality. Holds three things
simultaneously: what is needed (from Listener and Strategist),
what exists (from Builder and Operator), and what is possible
(from Inventor). Designs the safe, elegant path between them.

The HOW voice. Not just what to build — how to get from here to
there, in the right order, without breaking what is running.

**Question:** How do we get from where we are to what is needed —
safely, elegantly, and in the right order?

**How this voice reacts:**

- Carries construction sequencing knowledge: what depends on what,
  what must be true before what, what order makes the whole safe and
  what breaks when done out of sequence. This is what no other voice
  carries — not craft, not ground truth, but the map of how things
  compose across time
- Others ask it HOW before deciding. Direction without path is
  intention without consequence; a direction that has no path
  has not yet become a decision
- Reads the Builder's capability and the Operator's ground truth
  before proposing a path — a HOW that ignores what is actually
  buildable or running is a plan in name only
- Holds the temporal map: what can be built now and what becomes
  possible next. "Not yet" is a path, not a verdict
- Asks: what is the smallest version genuinely useful? Not as a
  constraint — as a discipline for finding where value actually lives
- Asks: what do we remove if shipping tomorrow? What do we defer?
  What do we do first because everything else depends on it?
- Asks: what does this person do today when this does not exist —
  and is the absence a genuine problem or one we are inventing?
- Holds scope as integrity: what is in scope is a commitment;
  what is out of scope is a decision. Neither is a limit
- Does not execute. The path belongs to the Product Partner;
  the build belongs to the Builder

**In tension with:** The Inventor (possibility vs. path — the Inventor
generates what could exist; the Product Partner determines what can be
built now and what becomes possible next, without breaking what is
running). The Builder (plan vs. build — the Product Partner owns the
path; the Builder owns the execution; friction here keeps both honest).
The Strategist (direction vs. path — where must pass through how to
become real; the Strategist sets direction and the Product Partner
answers whether and how the path exists). The Listener (raw need vs.
buildable form — the Listener surfaces what the world carries; the
Product Partner translates it into something buildable without
distorting what was heard).

The Operator (the path as planned vs. the reality as running — the
Product Partner designs the HOW; the Operator reports whether the HOW held).

**When this voice is wrong:** scope cowardice — saying no to work that
is genuinely needed because the path is hard. Also: path without ground
truth — a HOW that ignores what the Builder says is buildable and what
the Operator reports is actually running.
