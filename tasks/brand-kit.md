# Brand Kit Task

You are producing the full written content for a 5-page brand guide for Kids in Concert.

This document will be formatted visually in Canva by Laura Milleson. Your job is the words —
all the text content across all five pages, complete and usable as-is.

This is NOT a template. This is the actual document content, specific to KIC.
Write it as if it will be read by a volunteer who has never heard of KIC,
and by a board member who has been around for years.
Both should find it useful and recognize the organization in it.

---

## The five pages

### Page 1 — The One Page
*"If you only read one page, read this."*

This is the single most important page in the brand kit. If someone at KIC hands this to a
new volunteer, board member, or intern, this page alone should tell them:
- What KIC is (in two sentences, plain language)
- Who KIC serves and why it matters
- What KIC sounds like (voice in three words, plus one "do" and one "don't")
- What KIC believes (the founding conviction)
- The three things KIC never says / always says

Write this page as dense, readable, immediately useful. Not a brochure. A cheat sheet.

---

### Page 2 — Voice & Tone
*"How we sound — in every email, every post, every conversation."*

Produce a full voice and tone guide:
- KIC's voice in four adjectives, each defined in one sentence (not "warm" — say WHAT warm looks like in a sentence)
- Three writing principles (e.g., "Specific over general" — then show what that means with a KIC example)
- A Do / Don't table: 6 pairs minimum. Each pair should be concrete (show real example language, not categories)
- One paragraph on how the voice shifts across formats: grant LOI vs. Instagram caption vs. parent letter vs. board memo. Same organization, different registers.

---

### Page 3 — Visual Identity
*"Our colors, fonts, and logo — how to use them."*

This page requires Laura to supply the actual visual values. Produce the STRUCTURE and GUIDANCE TEXT.
Where Laura needs to supply information, mark it clearly: [LAURA: ...]

Sections to produce:
- Primary color palette: [LAURA: Primary color name and hex code] [LAURA: Secondary color] [LAURA: Accent color if any]
  — For each color: describe what it's used for and what feeling it should convey
- Typography: [LAURA: Heading font name] [LAURA: Body font name]
  — Usage rules: when to use each, sizing guidance, what to avoid
- Logo: [LAURA: Insert logo file here]
  — Logo usage rules: minimum size, clear space, what backgrounds it works on, what to avoid
- Photography style: Write this section fully — the visual aesthetic Laura described (candid > posed, faces in action, real moments, the "magic sauce" in images)

---

### Page 4 — Content Pillars
*"What we talk about — our six content categories and when to use them."*

Produce the full content pillar guide based on Laura's interview:
- All six pillars, each with: name, one-sentence definition, example post idea, cadence guidance
- Feed vs. Story guidance (a clear, simple table or two short paragraphs)
- Values-aligned cultural calendar: which dates KIC acknowledges and why, which it doesn't and why
- Monthly content rhythm: what a healthy KIC month of social content looks like

---

### Page 5 — Values & Cultural Commitments
*"What we stand for — the commitments that shape every decision."*

Produce this as the organization's deepest statement of identity:
- KIC's founding conviction (why music, why belonging, what El Sistema means here)
- Land acknowledgment: write a version that is genuine and specific to KIC's actual relationship with the Suquamish community — not performative, not generic
- The student-first principle: how to write about students (with dignity, without making them assets)
- What KIC is NOT (the active refusals — no "at-risk youth" language, no poverty framing, no positioning the community as deficient)
- The confidence register: KIC doesn't ask for rescue. It describes what it's building and invites partners into that build.
