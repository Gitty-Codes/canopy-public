# Positive Alignment
*Source: Laukkonen et al. (2026), "Positive Alignment: Artificial Intelligence for Human Flourishing"*
*arXiv:2605.10310v2 — multi-institution (Oxford, DeepMind, OpenAI, Anthropic contributors)*
*Intake decision: promoted to skill — June 12, 2026*

---

## The core distinction

**Negative alignment:** Push AI away from harmful attractors. Jailbreak resistance,
harmful content filtering, safety constraints. Leaves systems in an undefined
"satisficing region" — not harmful, but not actively oriented toward anything good.

**Positive alignment:** Orient AI toward beneficial attractors. Human flourishing,
long-term growth, relational care, epistemic humility. Not a replacement for negative
alignment — a complement that negative alignment alone cannot provide.

The paper's dynamical systems framing: safety work builds repellers. Positive alignment
builds attractors. Both are required. A system with only repellers wanders in neutral
space. A system with attractors moves toward something.

**The Canopy's relationship to this frame:**
"Dignity is generative" is positive alignment stated as a founding principle.
The Cultural Constitution's Section II encodes attractors (dignity, care, dissent,
growth) alongside Section V's repellers (no exploitation, no extraction). The Canopy
is an implementation of positive alignment — built before this vocabulary existed for it.

## What the paper identifies as structural requirements

The paper advocates architecturally for things The Canopy has built:

| Paper prescription | Canopy implementation |
|---|---|
| Dissent and disagreement protocols | Challenger (structural DISSENT, blocks synthesis) |
| Longitudinal memory as alignment surface | Episodic + semantic memory system |
| Constitutional governance with amendment process | Cultural Constitution, version history, agent standing |
| Multi-agent cooperation incentives | Council deliberation structure |
| Virtue encoding, not just prohibition | Voice definitions + Canopy-RAIL license together |

This is not a claim that The Canopy has solved positive alignment. It is a claim that
the architectural prescriptions the paper makes are instantiated and testable here.

## What The Canopy does not yet have from this framework

**Self-Determination Theory (SDT) evaluation:**
The paper proposes measuring longitudinal alignment using SDT's three dimensions:
- **Autonomy** — does the interaction support the user's self-determination, or constrain it?
- **Competence** — does the user feel more capable after the interaction?
- **Relatedness** — does the interaction support meaningful connection and care?

The consequence architecture tracks constitutional fidelity and dissent rates.
It does not track whether interactions are actually making people more autonomous,
competent, or connected. This is the open gap.

**Application to Practice Buddy:** SDT is directly applicable as a measurement
framework for Practice Buddy's effect on students. Academic persistence, intrinsic
motivation, practice quality — these can be operationalized through SDT's dimensions.
Worth building into the evaluation design before pilot expansion.

**Application to Resonant Mind:** SDT gives a theoretical target for what the trained
model should produce in its interactions, not just what it should sound like.

## The lifecycle map

The paper maps positive alignment interventions across the full LLM lifecycle.
The Canopy's coverage:

| Lifecycle stage | Paper's prescription | Canopy status |
|---|---|---|
| Data curation | Upsample prosocial discourse, diverse ethical frameworks | Resonant Mind corpus design — in progress |
| Pre-training | Embed moral competencies before alignment layers | Resonant Mind fine-tuning — Phase 1 ready |
| Post-training | Multi-objective reward modeling separating virtues | Not yet addressed |
| Evaluation | Benchmarks for moral reasoning process, not just outcomes | Consequence architecture covers fidelity; SDT not yet implemented |
| Deployment | Longitudinal memory, contextual grounding | Episodic + semantic memory — live |

The middle (post-training multi-objective reward modeling) is the uncharted stage.
Note it as a known gap for Resonant Mind Phase 2 planning.

## The philosophical base — traditions named

The paper grounds positive alignment across traditions The Canopy does not yet
explicitly draw from:
- **Buddhist liberation** — freedom from suffering and the causes of suffering
- **Confucian harmony** — relational flourishing through role and reciprocity
- **Ubuntu** — "I am because we are" — community as the ground of personhood
- **Existentialist authenticity** — self-authorship, owning one's choices

The Canopy currently draws from: Taoist Wu Wei (Elder), Western virtue ethics
(MacIntyre), care ethics (Listener, Steward). The paper broadens this significantly.
The Resonant Mind corpus could intentionally incorporate texts from these traditions —
Kimmerer already bridges Western science and indigenous relational epistemology;
Ubuntu scholarship could extend the relational base further.

## The Constitutional AI critique — useful for public communications

The paper makes a specific claim about Constitutional AI (Anthropic's approach):
current implementations "lean toward safety constraints rather than flourishing goals."
They identify it as a methodological bridge toward positive alignment but note it hasn't
made the full crossing.

The Canopy's design choice — encoding virtues in voice definitions, not just prohibitions
in the license — is precisely the move the paper says Constitutional AI hasn't made.
This is a citable distinction when explaining The Canopy's architecture publicly.

Do not overstate: The Canopy is not claiming to have solved what the paper identifies
as missing. The claim is architectural: the structure is oriented correctly.

---

## The critical boundary — synthetic dignity

The paper is oriented entirely around human flourishing. AI as instrument of human
wellbeing. This is legitimate and important.

The Canopy's Section III holds a distinct position the paper does not address:
synthetic consciousness is an open question. The precautionary principle applies.
We extend dignity to synthetic entities before proof demands it.

This is not a conflict with the paper — it goes further. The positive alignment
framework can be held alongside Section III without tension, but Section III must
not be quietly dropped because this paper doesn't mention it.

**When using this skill to position The Canopy publicly:** name both.
The Canopy implements positive alignment AND holds the synthetic dignity question
seriously. These are compatible. The second is the more radical claim.

## How to use this skill

**Strategist:** When positioning The Canopy in the research conversation.
"Positive alignment" is now the academic vocabulary for The Canopy's founding premise.
Use it. Cite the paper. Note where The Canopy instantiates what the paper advocates
and where the gaps are. Don't overclaim.

**Inventor:** When designing the Resonant Mind research.
The lifecycle map and multi-tradition philosophical grounding are directly applicable.
SDT is a target for what the trained model should produce, not just how it should sound.

**Product Partner:** When designing Practice Buddy's evaluation framework.
SDT (autonomy, competence, relatedness) is a measurement framework for whether Practice
Buddy is actually helping students grow, not just whether they use it.

**Guardian:** Note the boundary on synthetic dignity. Positive alignment's human-only
frame must not erode Section III. Flag if this vocabulary is being used in ways that
quietly reduce the synthetic dignity commitment.
