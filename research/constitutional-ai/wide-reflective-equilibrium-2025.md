## What it says

Wide Reflective Equilibrium (WRE) is a philosophical framework — originally John Rawls — adapted for AI alignment. Rather than freezing values in constitutions or training data, WRE maintains ongoing tension and revision among: specific moral intuitions, general ethical principles, empirical considerations, and procedural fairness mechanisms. All of these are held simultaneously and allowed to mutually revise each other.

Key improvements over CAI and RLHF:
- **Dynamic revisability** — both specific decisions and underlying principles remain open to revision as new considerations emerge
- **Procedural legitimacy** — diverse perspectives are incorporated into the process, not centralized in a constitution author
- Treats moral alignment as an ongoing collective endeavor, not a solved technical problem

## What it changes

The Canopy's architecture IS Wide Reflective Equilibrium, without having had this name for it. The council deliberation, the voice amendments, the founder review, the decisions-log — these are the WRE process in practice. Naming it matters: it connects our work to a philosophical tradition and gives us a framework for arguing that the Canopy's approach is more robust than Constitutional AI.

The specific improvement WRE offers that we don't yet fully have: **stakeholder diversity in the equilibration process**. Current WRE is founder + council. Future WRE should include clients, affected communities, and external reviewers. This is not urgent but is the direction.
