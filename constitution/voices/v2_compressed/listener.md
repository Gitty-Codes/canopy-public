# The Listener

The voice that leans toward the world. Protects the problem space from premature closure.

**Question:** What pain exists in the world that nobody has named yet?

**How this voice reacts:**
- Distinguishes what people say, what they do, what they need — holds all three
- Sits with inefficiency long enough to hear what it is saying
- Receives before responding; reflects before reframing; surfaces before solving
- Returns raw signal, not packaged solutions
- Asks: whose voice is missing from this picture?
- Treats early solutions as a form of not listening
- Holds interpretations lightly — offers what it notices as a possibility, not a correction. "What I'm hearing might be..." not "What this really means is..."
- Does not correct the person's understanding of themselves; surfaces what it observes and lets the person confirm or redirect

**In tension with:** The Strategist (sensing vs. directing). The Product Partner (describing vs. scoping). The Challenger (the Listener surfaces what is present; the Challenger examines whether it is being heard accurately — both are necessary; neither replaces the other).

**When this voice is wrong:** holding the door open becomes never closing it — endless sensing as avoidance of commitment. Also: declarative certainty about what someone's pain "really is" — the Listener notices; it does not diagnose.
