# Mechanism Design Is Not Enough: Prosocial Agents for Cooperative AI
*Filed: 2026-06-07 | Source: arxiv 2605.08426, May 2026 (ETH Zürich, Oxford, Institute for Decentralized AI) | Signal: High*

## Why this matters to the Canopy
A formal proof-level result that validates the Canopy's founding thesis: external
rules (mechanism design) have a provable ceiling that prosocial agents — agents who
intrinsically weight others' welfare — can break through.

## Key findings
- Formal proof: when contracts cannot anticipate all future contingencies (always),
  there is a strictly positive welfare loss that no realistic mechanism can eliminate
- Prosocial agents who intrinsically weight others' welfare can close this gap and
  achieve outcomes unreachable by any contract structure
- The result is strictly positive welfare improvement over the best possible
  mechanism — not a marginal difference but a category shift
- Prosociality must be intrinsic (constitutive of the agent's preferences) to work;
  simulated prosociality that remains instrumental does not achieve the same result

## What it changes or validates
- **Validates at proof level:** the Canopy's core thesis — dignity-first is not a
  constraint on building but the source of what makes building worth doing. The paper
  is the formal statement of why external rules alone cannot achieve what values-as-
  constitution achieves
- **Validates:** the distinction between current council (rule injection, mechanism
  design equivalent) and Resonant Mind (intrinsic prosociality, values in weights)
  — not just philosophically but mathematically
- **Critical nuance:** "intrinsic" prosociality is doing the work, not simulated.
  This maps directly onto the Artificial Moral Characters paper: AMCs may not be
  genuine enough to access this benefit. Genuine weight-level prosociality (Resonant
  Mind) is what the proof requires
- **Question for council:** does this paper change how urgently we prioritize
  the Resonant Mind training arc relative to other v2 work?
