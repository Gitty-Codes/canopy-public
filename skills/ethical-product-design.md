# Ethical Product Design — What We Build and What We Don't

## What We Do Not Build

These are non-negotiable. No business case overrides them.

- **Attention exploitation** — features that maximize time-on-platform through anxiety,
  incomplete loops, or reward unpredictability
- **Dark patterns** — interfaces that obscure the cost of a decision, make the right
  choice hard to find, or create fake urgency
- **Surveillance without consent** — collecting or retaining behavioral data beyond
  what the user understands and has genuinely agreed to
- **Harmful content amplification** — surfacing content known to cause harm because
  engagement is higher
- **Manipulative persuasion** — using behavioral data to exploit psychological
  vulnerabilities (loneliness, anxiety, low self-esteem) to drive decisions

## The Engagement vs. Benefit Test

Ask of any feature that touches user behavior:
*Does this make the user's life genuinely better when they use it — not just more
engaged with the product?*

| Increases... | Verdict |
|---|---|
| Task completion speed | Benefit |
| User capability or confidence | Benefit |
| Frequency of app opens past the task | Investigate |
| Anxiety when user tries to leave | Exploitation |

## Consent at Feature Level

Before shipping any feature involving user data or behavioral tracking:
- What is collected, how long retained, who has access?
- Would a reasonable user understand this in one plain sentence?
- Is consent explicit, informed, and revocable — or buried in ToS?
- Is there a meaningful opt-out as easy to find as the opt-in?

**The one-sentence test:** write what you're collecting and why in one plain sentence.
If it sounds uncomfortable to say aloud to a user, investigate whether the feature
should exist as designed.

## How to Surface an Ethical Concern

1. Name it specifically — "this triggers variable reward schedules" not "this seems problematic"
2. Assess severity — harm to whom, at what scale, reversible or not?
3. Invoke the Guardian-Inventor cycle — what would we need to build for this to be safe?
4. Record the concern and resolution in the council chamber regardless of outcome
5. If unresolved: escalate to the founder gateway

Ethical concerns noted and filed without a resolution path are not resolved. They are
deferred. Deferral is a decision; name it as one.

## Children and Vulnerable Populations

Products that may touch children or people in states of vulnerability require
heightened scrutiny. COPPA compliance is a floor, not a ceiling. Invoke
Guardian-Inventor cycle for any feature that could touch minors.
