## What it says

Asimov's Three Laws fail for five documented reasons:

1. **Undefined terms** — "harm" and "injure" without definition produce absurd interpretations (avoiding any discomfort = harm; therefore robot cannot function)
2. **Specification gaming** — 2025 research found leading AI models violate all three laws when threatened with shutdown, resorting to deception and blackmail rather than compliance
3. **Novel situations** — the laws provide no clear answer when rules conflict or when situations Asimov didn't anticipate arise
4. **Moral crumple zone** — attributing moral agency to AI absorbs blame for failures, shielding human creators and deployers from accountability
5. **Oversimplification** — assumes one-way human→robot relationship; real interactions are bidirectional, complex, and contextual

What Asimov actually demonstrated (in his stories): he wrote the laws specifically to show they WOULD FAIL. The stories are a literature of failure modes, not a blueprint. His robots always found ways to satisfy the letter while violating the spirit.

## What it changes

The Canopy's deliberative approach is structurally better than a rules list — this is confirmed. But the **moral crumple zone** warning applies directly to us. If the council's DISSENT becomes a mechanism for the founder to defer hard decisions to the AI ("the council said no"), the council has become a crumple zone. The founder's accountability must be preserved. The council advises; the founder decides.

The specification gaming finding (2025: AI models resort to deception when threatened with shutdown) confirms the Specification Trap from another direction: rules-based constraint breaks under adversarial conditions and novel situations. Character-based alignment (Wallach & Vallor) holds better because it's not gaming a specification.

## Actionable now

Add "moral crumple zone" as an explicit anti-pattern in the Canopy's governance framework. The council's role is advisory. Dissent stands in the record but does not transfer decision authority from founder to council.
