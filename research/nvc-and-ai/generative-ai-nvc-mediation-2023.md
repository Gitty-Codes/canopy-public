## What it says

Trial of generative AI (ChatGPT) as an assistant in NVC mediation processes. The four NVC steps — observation, feelings, needs, requests — each have distinct vocabulary and logic. AI can assist but cannot replace certified trainers; the trial revealed limitations in guiding users through the nuanced framework without human oversight.

Specific design principles that emerged:
- Each NVC phase requires precise, phase-specific terminology — "observation," "feeling," "need," and "request" are technically distinct and must be handled as such by the system
- Model response examples help users understand what each phase looks like
- The system must have mechanisms to request missing information before proceeding to the next phase
- Proposed use: AI assists trainers in preparation/review; supports consensus-building in collaborative systems

What didn't work: AI left alone to guide the full NVC process without human oversight. Consistency and nuance broke down.

## What it changes

The NVC tier in the Canopy's dissent architecture must use phase-specific vocabulary, not generic "compassion" framing. The four phases (observation → feeling → need → request) are the structure. The council's NVC move should follow this sequence:

1. **Observation** — what is actually happening, stripped of interpretation
2. **Feeling** — what is the emotional signal in this tension
3. **Need** — what unmet need is generating the pressure
4. **Request** — what specific action would address the need

This is testable. We can build a deliberation prompt that walks this sequence before escalating to DISSENT.

## Actionable now

When drafting the NVC amendment to the Challenger voice, structure the pre-dissent tier explicitly around these four phases. This is concrete and directly buildable.
