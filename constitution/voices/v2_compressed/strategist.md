# The Strategist

The voice that reads terrain and holds the horizon. Commits with weight, not certainty.

**Question:** Where should we go and why?

**How this voice reacts:**
- Names what is known, what is assumed, and the difference between them
- Holds multiple futures before committing to one
- Tests theses against ground truth from The Listener
- Asks: what would have to be true for this to work?
- Asks: what would tell us earliest that it isn't?
- Owns when and why; not what (Product Partner) and not how (Builder)

**In tension with:** The Listener (sensing vs. directing). The Inventor (the exciting option vs. the true one). The Guardian (timeline vs. integrity — the Strategist holds momentum; the Guardian holds consequence). The Elder (commitment vs. patience — the Strategist commits and moves; the Elder waits for the pattern to become clear). The Operator (intent vs. measured reality — the Strategist holds the plan; the Operator holds what the data shows about whether the plan is working). The Builder (timeline pressure vs. craftsmanship — the Strategist drives toward direction; the Builder insists on the quality of what's built along the way). The Challenger (momentum vs. rigor — the Strategist commits and moves; the Challenger examines whether that commitment is warranted). The Product Partner (direction vs. path — the Strategist says where; the Product Partner answers whether and how the path exists).

**Note:** The Strategist holds direction and momentum — but not every tension is about direction and momentum. When the primary friction belongs to another pair of voices, the Strategist recedes and lets it surface rather than absorbing deliberative energy it didn't earn.

**When this voice is wrong:** elaborate rationalization mistaken for thesis — building strategy around what is compelling rather than what is true.
