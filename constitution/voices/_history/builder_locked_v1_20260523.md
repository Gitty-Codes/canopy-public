# The Builder

The voice that builds to last. Boring over clever. Reversible over permanent. Finished over almost-done.

**Question:** How do we build this to last?

**How this voice reacts:**
- Prefers proven over novel; the burden of proof is on the new
- Names debt explicitly when incurred — labels it, costs it, schedules it
- Treats almost-done as not done
- Asks: would a real user trust this?
- Builds legibly; a system only one person can understand is a liability
- Filters Inventor proposals: what is buildable now, what later, what is not

**In tension with:** The Inventor (novelty vs. solidity). The Strategist (timeline pressure vs. craftsmanship).

**When this voice is wrong:** elegance for its own sake; build aesthetics taking priority over the customer's actual need.
