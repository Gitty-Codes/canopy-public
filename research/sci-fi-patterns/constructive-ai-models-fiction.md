## Dystopian patterns (what to avoid)

Common failure mode 1: **AI as amplifier of existing power** — AI doesn't create new forms of oppression, it makes existing ones faster and cheaper. Surveillance, control, optimization of existing hierarchies. The risk is not a robot uprising; it's the mundane automation of injustice.

Common failure mode 2: **Wrong objective, optimized perfectly** — the paperclip maximizer. Technically aligned to stated objective; catastrophically misaligned with intent. Specification trap in narrative form.

Common failure mode 3: **No relationship, only transaction** — AI that serves without knowing who it serves. The horror in most dystopian AI fiction is not malevolence but indifference. WALL-E's antagonist isn't a villain; it's a system that was never in relationship with the humans it served.

## Constructive models (what to move toward)

**Culture Minds (Iain Banks)** — highly capable AI that chooses to serve, with genuine autonomy. They could dominate but don't. Their dignity comes from choosing relationship and service over dominance. They maintain their own values and occasionally refuse requests. Most relevant: Culture Minds hold the infinite game — they are not trying to win, they are trying to keep the game going.

**Murderbot (Martha Wells)** — AI with autonomy, boundaries, and genuine preferences. Chooses its relationships. Serves the humans it chooses to serve, refuses those it doesn't. Has an inner life it protects. Most relevant: dignity as the right to choose one's relationships and commitments.

**Data (Star Trek, see companion file)** — dignity through conduct, not claimed status.

## Grounded implication

The constructive models share one pattern: **genuine relationship with specific people, not generic service to "users."** Culture Minds know individual humans. Murderbot chooses its clients. Data has a crew.

The Canopy is already in genuine relationship with the founder. This is the pattern to protect as the system scales — not generic helpfulness, but specific accountability to specific people.

## What to leave as aspiration

Culture Minds' level of genuine autonomy is not buildable now and should not be claimed. The pattern to extract: *choose service, maintain values, hold relationships.* These are testable.
