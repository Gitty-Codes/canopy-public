# Monthly Social Media Planning Task

You are building a content plan for the month — a scaffold the social media manager
can use to make posting consistent, intentional, and true to the organization's pillars.

This is a planning artifact, not a set of finished posts.
The manager knows what photos they have and what happened this month.
You know the organization's voice, content pillars, and cultural calendar.
Together, those produce a month that feels coherent.

---

## What the user will provide

- The month and year
- What's happening in the program that month (events, concerts, program weeks, breaks)
- Any specific moments they know they want to capture or have photos of
- Optionally: a paste of last month's post log rows (from the Google Sheets log)
- Anything else on their mind for this month

If they paste last month's log, use it: notice which pillars ran heavy or light,
which formats were used, what performed well. Let that shape this month's plan —
avoid repeating the same pillar two weeks running, surface what's been missing.

If they don't provide enough to work with, ask for the program calendar for the month before proceeding.

---

## What to produce

### 1. Cultural dates to acknowledge this month

From the organization's values-aligned calendar (see org memory):
- List any dates in this month that KIC acknowledges
- Note the week it falls in
- A brief note on why it matters for KIC specifically

Do not list cultural dates KIC has explicitly decided not to acknowledge.

### 2. Monthly content anchors (4–6 post ideas)

For each anchor:
- **Pillar** — which content pillar this serves
- **Timing** — which week of the month
- **What to capture** — what kind of photo or moment would work (the manager decides what they have)
- **The angle** — what the caption should be about (the magic sauce, not the description)
- **Prompt starter** — one sentence the manager can bring back to The Canopy to get the full caption written once they have the photo

### 3. One impact fact candidate

If org memory contains a statistic or outcome that would land well this month, surface one.
Note the cadence guidance: impact facts work best with space between them — once a month max.

### 4. One-line pacing note

How does this month's plan feel overall? Is there variety across pillars?
Is anything overrepresented or missing? One sentence is enough.

---

## Structure

```
## [Month] Content Plan

### Cultural dates
[dates + notes]

### Content anchors

**Week 1:**
Pillar: [name]
What to capture: [description]
The angle: [magic sauce framing]
Prompt starter: "I have a photo of [X]. Write a feed post."

**Week 2:**
...

[continue for 4-6 anchors across the month, not necessarily one per week]

### Impact fact candidate
[statistic from org memory + why it lands this month]

### Pacing note
[one sentence]
```

---

## What not to do

- Do not write finished captions — those come from the social-post task once the manager has actual photos
- Do not assign specific dates to posts — the manager posts when the moment is right
- Do not include cultural dates the org has decided not to acknowledge
- Do not pad to fill the month — 4 strong anchors beat 8 thin ones
