# SpeakSoftly: Scaffolding Nonviolent Communication in Intimate Relationships through LLM-Powered Just-In-Time Interventions
*Filed: 2026-06-07 | Source: arxiv 2604.05382, April 2026 | Signal: Medium-High*

## Why this matters to the Canopy
The closest published system-level implementation of the full NVC cycle to the
Canopy's Challenger architecture — tested empirically in high-stakes live dialogue.

## Key findings
- Implements full NVC observation→feeling→need→request cycle computationally
- Two components: NVC-Prompt (detects verbal aggression, proposes de-escalation
  reformulations) and NVC-Guide (surfaces user's underlying feelings and needs)
- Three intervention modes tested: Basic Reminder, Neutral Guide, Empathetic Guide
- Mixed-methods study with 18 couples in simulated and live conflict settings
- Key empirical finding: intervention depth and tone each matter independently —
  not just presence/absence of NVC framing

## What it changes or validates
- **Validates:** the Challenger's curiosity tier (what need does this tension
  protect?) maps onto NVC-Guide's "uncover feelings and needs" phase; the
  precision tier (what would meet the need without the cost?) maps onto
  NVC-Prompt's "suggest revision" phase
- **Empirical insight:** depth and tone matter independently — the Challenger
  should vary both depending on the stakes and register of the deliberation, not
  just whether NVC framing is present
- **Caveat:** this is human-to-human mediation; translation to multi-agent
  AI deliberation requires a conceptual step
- **Runner-up noted:** "Toward Needs-Conscious Design" (arxiv 2508.11149) introduces
  "Empathy Fog" — AI mediation creates uncertainty about how much genuine human
  effort was invested. Worth filing if Canopy human-in-the-loop questions arise.
